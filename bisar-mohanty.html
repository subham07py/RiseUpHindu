The Legend of Bisar Mohanty: A Heroic Saga of Devotion and Defiance in Odisha

Renowned as an "El Dorado" for its wealth and resources, Odisha attracted the ambitions of neighboring powers, particularly the Sultanate of Bengal under Sulaiman Karrani. The period was marked by intense political rivalries and territorial ambitions, with the Mughal emperor Akbar expanding his empire from Delhi and the Afghan sultans of Bengal vying for regional dominance.

Mukunda Dev faced numerous challenges, both internal and external. Internally, treachery and rebellion plagued his court, exemplified by the betrayal of feudatory chief Ramachandra Bhanja, who declared himself king during a critical moment. Externally, Sulaiman Karrani‚Äôs desire to annex Odisha led to a series of military campaigns. Mukunda Dev‚Äôs alliance with Akbar, formed to counter potential threats from Bengal, further enraged Sulaiman, setting the stage for a devastating invasion in 1568 AD.

The Rise of Kalapahad: From Brahmin to Destroyer

Central to this narrative is the figure of Kalapahad, whose transformation from a Hindu Brahmin to a feared iconoclast is a pivotal element of the legend. According to historical accounts and Odia folklore, Kalapahad was originally Rajiv Lochan Roy (or Kalachand Roy Bhadury), a Bengali Brahmin and general in Mukunda Dev‚Äôs army. Sulaiman Karrani, after suffering defeat in an earlier battle, devised a diplomatic strategy to undermine Odisha‚Äôs defenses. This conversion marked a turning point, as Kalapahad became a zealous destroyer of Hindu temples and a symbol of terror across Bengal, Bihar, and Odisha.

Scholars like Prof. P. Mukherjee have questioned the romantic narrative, suggesting Kalapahad was a "full-blooded Afghan" rather than a converted Brahmin. However, the Odia tradition, as recorded in texts like the Madalapanji (the Jagannath Temple chronicle), firmly holds to the story of his conversion and subsequent iconoclastic campaign, making it a cornerstone of the legend.

The Invasion of Odisha and the Fall of Mukunda Dev

In 1568 AD, while Akbar was preoccupied with the siege of Chitor, Sulaiman Karrani launched a two-pronged invasion of Odisha. One army was led by Sulaiman himself, while the other was commanded by his son Bayazid and Kalapahad.
