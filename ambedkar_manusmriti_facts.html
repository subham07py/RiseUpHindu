Did Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Burn Manusmriti?

It is widely believed ‚Äî and even celebrated in some circles ‚Äî that Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar publicly burned the Manusmriti (an ancient Hindu legal text) on December 25, 1927, as a symbolic act against caste oppression. This event is often cited online, and many groups even observe Manusmriti Dahan Diwas (Manusmriti Burning Day) annually. But what is the truth?

Let's explore the documented history, speeches, and publications ‚Äî especially from the Collected Works of Dr. Ambedkar (Vol. 38, Hindi Edition) ‚Äî and find out if this event really happened the way it's told.

üìú The Mahad Satyagraha ‚Äì The Real Context

Before jumping to conclusions, it's important to understand the broader context of what happened at Mahad in 1927.

  • The Mahad Satyagraha was organized by the Mahad Satyagraha Parishad on December 25‚Äì27, 1927.
  • Dr. Ambedkar was invited by the Parishad Secretary Sitaram Namdev Shivtarkar to address the gathering.
  • The goal of the Satyagraha was to demand equal access to public water sources and social equality.

Yes, Manusmriti burning happened at this event ‚Äî but not by Dr. Ambedkar himself.

No. He did not.

In the pamphlet Dr. Ambedkar published before the Mahad Satyagraha, there is no mention of Manusmriti Dahan.

  • Dr. Ambedkar's entire speech made no mention of Manusmriti, nor did he ever utter the word "Manusmriti" during the talk.

üìå No source or eyewitness account confirms that Dr. Ambedkar was present at the burning, or that he lit the fire.

Dr. Ambedkar mentioned Manusmriti Dahan only twice:

  1. In the newspaper Bahishkrit Bharat on February 3, 1928

üìå In both instances, Dr. Ambedkar never claimed he burned the text himself.

‚úÖ Conclusion: What Do the Facts Show?

  • üìå Dr. B.R. Ambedkar did not burn the Manusmriti on December 25, 1927
