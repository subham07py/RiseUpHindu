The Vedas

The Vedas are the oldest sacred texts of Hinduism, considered eternal divine knowledge (Shruti) revealed to ancient sages. They form the foundation of Hindu philosophy, rituals, and spirituality.

Rig Veda

The oldest Veda containing hymns dedicated to various deities. It consists of 1,028 hymns divided into 10 mandalas.

Explore

Atharva Veda

Contains spells, charms, and incantations for daily life along with philosophical hymns. It's considered the knowledge storehouse of atharvanas.

Explore

Sama Veda

The "Veda of melodies" containing musical notations of Rig Vedic hymns. It forms the basis of Indian classical music.

Explore

Yajur Veda

The Veda of rituals and ceremonies, containing prose mantras for worship. It has both Krishna (black) and Shukla (white) recensions.

Explore

The Upavedas

Upavedas are auxiliary Vedas that deal with applied knowledge. Each Veda has an Upaveda associated with it, forming the basis of various arts and sciences.

Ayurveda

The science of life and longevity, associated with the Rig Veda. It encompasses holistic healing through herbs, diet, and lifestyle.

Explore

Dhanurveda

The martial arts and military science, associated with the Yajur Veda. It covers archery, weaponry, and combat strategies.

Explore

Gandharveda

The art of music, dance, and performing arts, associated with the Sama Veda. It's the foundation of Indian classical arts.

Explore

Silpaveda

The science of architecture and crafts, associated with the Atharva Veda. It includes Vastu Shastra and temple architecture.

Explore

Arthaveda

The science of economics, statecraft, and governance. It deals with wealth creation and social organization.
